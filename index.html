About Me

Welcome to my first page...EVER! I'm excited to show you what I can do. As my abilities evolve, over the course of my learning experience at Northwestern's Coding Bootcamp, I will be updating this page to show you all of my new skills. I'm a complete noob, so I welcome all the constructive criticism you have to offer.

My background is primarily in healthcare. I have been a nurse for 8 years and involved with healthcare for the last 10. I am currently a Clinical Research Nurse for division of Infectious Diseases at Northwestern Univerisity. My work is primarily on HIV/AIDS clinical trials. I plan to use all the fun stuff I learn during the next 6 months to help us get rid of a disease that has been affecting MY community for the last 30 plus years.

On an even more personal level, I call myself a Chicagoan because I've spent the bulk of my life here. I love this city and no matter how many times I've left, it always pulls me back. I run my days pretty full between full time work, working out, community involvement, and now class. I also like to have a little fun playing softball, rugby, and following the Chicago House music scene.

I am excited for you to follow me on this journey as I learn a new language. Let's push things forward!
